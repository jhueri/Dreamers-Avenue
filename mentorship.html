Dreamers Avenue Mentorship Program

The Dreamers Avenue Mentorship Program empowers undocumented students by providing personalized guidance and support throughout the college application process.

Cohort 1 Applications are now Closed

2025 Cohort Timeline

We run the mentorship program in three cohorts per year. Each cohort lasts 3 months

Cohort 1: April 1 - June 30

Cohort 2: August 1 - October 31

Cohort 3: December 1 - February 28

How To Apply

For Mentors

Want to make a real difference in a student’s life? Become a mentor.

We're looking for people who:

  • Understand or are passionate about the challenges undocumented students face
  • Have experience with the U.S. college application process
  • Are committed to meeting regularly and offering consistent, compassionate support

Applications Include:

  • Your background and relevant experience
  • Why you want to mentor
  • What areas you'd like to support (essays, financial aid, college search, etc.)
Apply to be a Mentor

For Mentees

If you’re an undocumented student in the U.S. and planning to apply to college, you’re eligible to apply.

To apply, you'll need to share:

  • A bit about your background and goals
  • Your academic interests
  • Any challenges you're facing with the college process

Once you apply, we’ll evaluate based on eligibility and match you with a mentor who fits your interests and needs.

Apply to be a Mentee

The Matching Process

We match mentors and mentees based on:

  • Academic interests
  • Career goals
  • Specific areas of support

Both mentee and mentor will receive an email with their match, and both must confirm before the pairing is official. If a match doesn’t work out, we’ll do our best to rematch within the cohort.

Meeting Expectations

Time Commitment

  • Meet at least 6 times over 3 months through video conferencing
  • Each meeting lasts 30 mins to 2 hours, depending on what's needed
