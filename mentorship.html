Mentorship Program

Connecting undocumented students with mentors who understand their unique challenges

Program Overview

Our mentorship program pairs undocumented high school students with trained mentors who guide them through every step of the college application process. Mentors provide personalized support, share their own experiences, and help students navigate the unique challenges they face.

The program runs from September to May each year, with applications opening in July. Mentors and mentees meet weekly, either virtually or in person, and participate in monthly group workshops on topics like essay writing, scholarship applications, and financial planning.

How It Works

1

Application

Students and potential mentors complete an online application sharing their background, interests, and goals.

2

Matching

Our team carefully matches mentees with mentors based on academic interests, career goals, and personal compatibility.

3

Orientation

Mentors and mentees attend orientation sessions to learn about program expectations and build initial connections.

4

Weekly Check-ins

Mentors and mentees meet weekly to work on college applications, discuss challenges, and track progress.

5

Monthly Workshops

All participants attend monthly workshops on key topics like essay writing, financial aid, and interview skills.

6

Celebration

We celebrate college acceptances and program completion with a special ceremony in May.

What Mentors Do

Commit Time

Meet with mentees weekly (1-2 hours) and attend monthly workshops (3 hours).

Guide Applications

Help with college selection, application forms, and essay writing.

Navigate Financial Aid

Assist with scholarship searches and alternative funding options.

Provide Support

Offer emotional encouragement and help mentees build confidence.

Frequently Asked Questions

When are applications due?

Applications for both mentors and mentees open July 1st and close August 15th each year. The program runs from September through May.

What is the time commitment?

Mentors and mentees meet weekly for 1-2 hours and attend monthly workshops (approximately 3 hours each). The total commitment is about 8-12 hours per month.

Do I need to disclose my immigration status?

No. While our program is designed for undocumented students, we never require disclosure of immigration status. All information shared is kept strictly confidential.

What qualifications do mentors need?

Mentors should have completed at least two years of college, be familiar with the college application process, and ideally have experience working with high school students. We provide comprehensive training for all mentors.

Is there a cost to participate?

No. Our mentorship program is completely free for mentees. We are funded through donations and grants to ensure all services are accessible.
