Mentorship Program

Connecting undocumented students with mentors who understand their unique challenges

Program Overview

Our mentorship program pairs undocumented high school students with experienced mentors who guide them through every step of the college application process. Mentors provide personalized support, share their own experiences, and help students navigate the unique challenges they face.

How It Works

1

Application

Students and potential mentors complete an online application sharing their background, interests, and goals.

2

Matching

Our team carefully matches mentees with mentors based on academic interests, career goals, and personal compatibility.

3

Orientation

Mentors and mentees attend orientation sessions to learn about program expectations and build initial connections.

4

Weekly Check-ins

Mentors and mentees meet weekly to work on college applications, discuss challenges, and track progress.

Cohort Schedule

We run three mentorship cohorts each year. Each cohort lasts 3 months.

  • Cohort 1: April – June
  • Cohort 2: August – October
  • Cohort 3: December – February

Applications open about one month before each cohort begins. Follow us on Instagram or check this page for updates!

Frequently Asked Questions

When are applications due?

Applications for each cohort open before the cohort starts. Deadlines are shared on our website and Instagram. If you're unsure, feel free to message us—we're happy to help!

What is the time commitment?

Mentees and mentors typically meet 2–3 times per month for 30–60 minutes. The full program lasts 3 months, and you can adjust your meeting schedule based on what works best for you both.

What qualifications do mentors need?

Mentors should be current college students or recent graduates who have experience applying to college. Compassion, commitment, and a willingness to guide others are what matter most.

Is there a cost to participate?

No, the program is completely free for both mentees and mentors. Dreamers Avenue is a volunteer-run nonprofit dedicated to making college access resources available to all undocumented students.
